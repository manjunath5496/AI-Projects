<h2 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">List of artificial intelligence projects</h2>
<h3><span id="Brain-inspired" class="mw-headline">Brain-inspired</span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a title="Blue Brain Project" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blue_Brain_Project">Blue Brain Project</a>, an attempt to create a synthetic brain by&nbsp;<a title="Reverse engineering" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reverse_engineering">reverse-engineering</a>&nbsp;the mammalian brain down to the molecular level.</li>
<li><a title="Google Brain" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_Brain">Google Brain</a>&nbsp;A deep learning project part of&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="Google X" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_X">Google X</a>&nbsp;attempting to have intelligence similar or equal to human-level.</li>
<li><a title="Human Brain Project" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_Brain_Project">Human Brain Project</a></li>
<li><a title="Numenta" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Numenta#The_NuPIC_Open_Source_Project">NuPIC</a>, an open source implementation by&nbsp;<a title="Numenta" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Numenta">Numenta</a>&nbsp;of its cortical learning algorithm.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span id="Cognitive_architectures" class="mw-headline">Cognitive architectures</span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a title="4CAPS" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/4CAPS">4CAPS</a>, developed at&nbsp;<a title="Carnegie Mellon University" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carnegie_Mellon_University">Carnegie Mellon University</a>&nbsp;under&nbsp;<a title="Marcel Just" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marcel_Just">Marcel A. Just</a></li>
<li><a title="ACT-R" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ACT-R">ACT-R</a>, developed at Carnegie Mellon University under&nbsp;<a title="John Robert Anderson (psychologist)" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Robert_Anderson_(psychologist)">John R. Anderson</a>.</li>
<li><a title="AIXI" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AIXI">AIXI</a>, Universal Artificial Intelligence developed by&nbsp;<a title="Marcus Hutter" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marcus_Hutter">Marcus Hutter</a>&nbsp;at&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="IDSIA" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IDSIA">IDSIA</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a title="Australian National University" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Australian_National_University">ANU</a>.</li>
<li><a title="CALO" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CALO">CALO</a>, a DARPA-funded, 25-institution effort to integrate many artificial intelligence approaches (natural language processing,&nbsp;<a title="Speech recognition" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Speech_recognition">speech recognition</a>,&nbsp;<a title="Machine vision" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Machine_vision">machine vision</a>,&nbsp;<a title="Probabilistic logic" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Probabilistic_logic">probabilistic logic</a>,&nbsp;<a title="Planning" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planning">planning</a>,&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="Reasoning" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reasoning">reasoning</a>, many forms of&nbsp;<a title="Machine learning" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Machine_learning">machine learning</a>) into an AI assistant that learns to help manage your office environment.</li>
<li><a title="CHREST" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CHREST">CHREST</a>, developed under&nbsp;<a title="Fernand Gobet" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fernand_Gobet">Fernand Gobet</a>&nbsp;at&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="Brunel University" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brunel_University">Brunel University</a>&nbsp;and Peter C. Lane at the&nbsp;<a title="University of Hertfordshire" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Hertfordshire">University of Hertfordshire</a>.</li>
